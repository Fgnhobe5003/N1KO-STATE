## 🛠️ How to Use the App

Once you launch the app, a small icon appears in your menu bar at the top of your screen. Click this icon to open the main window. You will see a list of your hardware components. Each section shows a live graph and current numbers.

The app updates every few seconds. You can change how often it refreshes by selecting the Settings menu inside the application. The menu bar icon also shows a quick snapshot of your CPU usage. You do not need to keep the main window open at all times. The app continues to monitor your system in the background even if you close the window.

## 📊 Understanding Your Data

N1KO-STATE translates complex hardware data into simple views. 

**CPU:** The processor handles your tasks. High numbers mean your computer works hard. If numbers stay high for a long time, check your running apps for issues.

**Memory:** This shows how much of your total RAM you use. If memory stays near 90 percent, your computer may feel slow. Closing unused tabs or programs helps clear space.

**Temperature:** Sensors inside your computer track heat. High temperatures are normal under heavy load. If the temperature stays high while your computer sits idle, ensure your air vents remain clear. 

**Fans:** Fans spin faster to cool the system when temperatures rise. High fan speeds create noise. You will notice faster fan speeds during gaming or video editing.

**GPU:** The graphics card handles images and videos. High GPU usage is common when you play games or edit photos.

## ❓ Common Questions

**Does this app collect my data?**
No. N1KO-STATE runs locally on your machine. It does not send information to any server.

**Can I hide the menu bar icon?**
If you prefer not to see the icon, you can set the app to show only the information within the main window through the settings menu.

**Will this app drain my battery?**
The app is built to use very little power. It stays quiet in the background and consumes minimal system resources. 

**What if the app does not launch?**
Check your security settings. Go to System Settings, then Privacy and Security. Look for a message about N1KO-STATE and click Open Anyway if your system blocked the app.

**How do I update the app?**
Visit the release link again to see if a newer version is available. Simply replace your current app with the new version from the website.
